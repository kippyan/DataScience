        1.   Assignment Information

        1. Due Date
          Friday, October 11, 2019 11:59 PM
          Points Possible
          100

        2. For the first deliverable, you will set up your portfolio so that you can publish your work continuously as you make progress. Getting Started (below) will give you directions for doing so. 

          Your first deliverable will concentrate on the Discovery and Data Preparation phases in the Data Analytics Life Cycle (see week 1 reading to review). In your R Notebook, you should document your process and provide R code for importing and cleaning your data as well as performing some initial investigation and demonstrating familiarity with the data.

          1. In an Introduction, describe your motivation for choosing the general topic of your portfolio.
          2. Identify your initial data set that you will be using. The data set should have at least:
            • at least 1000 observations
            • at least 10 variables
            • ...of which at least 3 are categorical
            • ...and at least 7 are continuous
          3. Describe your source, using critical analysis to assess the quality (and possible limitations) of it
          4. Document your variables and describe in plain language what they mean and how they are represented
          5. Document any manipulations you make to clean your data and organize it as Tidy Data as best you can prepare it for data analysis and modeling
          6. Summarize your data using descriptive statistics, along with visualizations (with accompanying interpretations) that help explore what possible insights you might investigate.
          7. Finally, describe what research questions you hope to explore in later phases, along with potential social & ethical implications of your work. For help identifying and phrasing research questions, see How to Write a Research Question

          Submitting your work

          Commit your work to your GitHub repository and verify that when browsing your url (e.g. https://yourusername.github.io/DataScience), that your published work matches what you intended.

          When browsing the first deliverable page, print it as PDF and submit the PDF by uploading it for this assignment. In addition, include links (as a Text Submission) to your GitHub Pages published website AND your GitHub repository.

          Grading

          Your work will be evaluated on an 100 point scale, based on the following rubric:

          • 20% Discovery - the deliverable shows a strong grasp of the activities and goals of Discovery stage with explanations that can be understood by the general public
          • 20% Data Preparation - the data has been cleaned appropriately and organized according to "Tidy Data" principles; the process has been explained in a clear and accurate manner
          • 20% R proficiency - the code blocks demonstrate a strong grasp of R for Data Science along with following best practices to make the code easy to understand and reproducible.
          • 20% Information Visualization - visualizations have been designed and interpreted well to communicate an accurate depiction of the data summary
          • 10% Critical thinking - the documentation and descriptions demonstrate critical thinking about the subject matter, including considerations for ethical and social implications as well as identifying opportunities for data-driven insights
          • 10% Communication - the combination of writing and visualizations help communicate a cohesive narrative that the general public could follow and find interesting
